Morf

“I love the streets...
It's the best place to develop as a musician.”

Inspired by Melbourne artist Dub FX; Morf, a vocal loopist from the UK decided to quit the job that got him to Australia to pursuit music. He started busking as a part time activity and slowly grew into a passion, a full time job and live from it. Even though it hasn’t been all that easy, it's the passion for what he does that keeps him going.

Beto Chinguel

“I fell in love with the pan pipes.
I never forgot that feeling and I kept playing them.”

The day came when a peruvian folk band played a gig at Beto’s hometown in the north of Peru. The sound of the pan flute and pan pipes got him. Years later, he left home, traveled around Peru and South America playing folk music, where he meet other musicians and eventually formed a folk band. Beto enjoys the feeling and connection with his native instruments.

George Kamikawa

“Music is sitll an enternaiment and, I like to entertain people.”

From a young age George discovered hit tracks from The Beatles and The Rolling Stones. Over the years, this blues/country frontman develop his music style that includes harmonica and kick drums, “kind of like a one man band...”, he jokes. Music should entertain, he believes, and decides to play fast-catchy tunes, even though he also enjoys slow ballads too.

La Rumba

“Music is the most real magic we have...
It's magical without being a trick.”

From Fitzroy and Brunswick, this band has a strong Gypsy, Flamingo and Latin American sound that comes from their various music instruments. Acoustics guitars, bongos, shakers, congas… Besides the cheerful tunes, they all love different music genres. “We have a love for it, and we are very receptive to all different types of music”, says uruguayan member and vocalist, Leo Salvo.

Gareth Weicko

“Doing this on the streets is creating that bridge for people to open their eyes towards classical music.”

Originally from Wales, Gareth traveled to Australia sold his car and purchased a piano. He then hitchhiked his way from Crains to Melbourne playing the keyboard, which he learned how to play when he was 12 years old."I feel quite complete when I do it, so it's like a therapy for me", he says.

Joel Bardwell

“The interaction you have with people is so special...
A whole lot of formalities go out the window.”

Since he was a child, Joel was interested in music and originally started by playing piano. He later taught himself different music instruments. Double bass, mandolin and accordion just to name a few. The "busking bug", as he calls it, hit him while waiting for a friend at a tram stop playing some tunes. After that, it never stopped. Joel is also a photographer with a registered business, but he still prefers performing music over the camera.
